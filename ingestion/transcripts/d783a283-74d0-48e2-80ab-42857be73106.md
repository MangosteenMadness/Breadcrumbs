## SYPL1 Spatial Expression Across Cell Types and MOSAIC Indications

The dotplot above displays SYPL1 expression from Visium spatial transcriptomics data (MOSAIC, baseline primary tumor + metastatic site samples, n = 77 patients across 8 indications). Dot size = % spots expressing; dot color = mean log-normalized expression.

### Key Observations

**Highest expression — Malignant cells:**
- **HNSC** (head & neck SCC): highest overall — 76% expressing, mean 3.76 (log-norm)
- **BLCA** (bladder cancer): 67.6% expressing, mean 3.17
- **OV** (high-grade serous carcinoma): 73% expressing, mean 3.04
- **MESO** (mesothelioma): 52.8% expressing, mean 2.19
- **NSCLC**: 49.3%, mean 2.01; **BRCA**: 46.4%, mean 2.13
- **DLBCL** malignant spots: comparatively low (20.7%, mean 1.08)

**Epithelial cell types:**
- *Epithelial_other* is notably high in **OV** (~78%, mean 3.44) and **BLCA** (72%, mean 3.69)
- *Epithelial_secretory* peaks in **OV** (75.4%, mean 3.12) and **NSCLC** (26.4%, mean 1.13)

**Stromal/immune cell types:**
- **MoMac**: highest in HNSC (62.9%, mean 3.12), substantially lower in NSCLC (17.3%, mean 0.76) — all pairwise contrasts highly significant (adj. p < 0.0001)
- **Fibroblasts**: peak in HNSC (56.4%, mean 2.74), low in GBM (~5%, mean 0.26)
- **Plasma cells**: peak in HNSC (53.4%, mean 2.68) and DLBCL T/NK (55.8%, mean 2.30)
- **T_NK**: high in DLBCL (55.8%, mean 2.30) and HNSC (48.7%, mean 2.48); low in GBM (<10%)
- **Endothelial**: moderate in HNSC (35%, mean 1.78) and GBM (36.9%, mean 1.55); low in NSCLC (<9%)

**Brain-specific cell types (GBM only):**
- Oligodendrocytes (43.2%, mean 1.81), Excitatory neurons (44.9%, mean 1.75), Microglia (41.9%, mean 1.77), and Astrocytes (33.3%, mean 1.44) show moderate SYPL1 expression, consistent with known synaptogyrin biology.

### Statistical significance
Pairwise Wilcoxon tests (Bonferroni-corrected) confirm that most cross-indication differences are highly significant (★★★★, adj. p < 0.0001), particularly for Malignant, Fibroblast, MoMac, T_NK, and Endothelial cell populations. HNSC systematically ranks highest for most stromal and immune cell types.

SYPL1 spatial expression analysis (sparse_aggregation_analysis, dotplot) was performed on MOSAIC Visium spatial transcriptomics data (77 patients, 8 indications: BLCA, BRCA, DLBCL, GBM, HNSC, MESO, NSCLC, OV). ### Biological interpretation

### Figure 2 — Immune Gene Dotplot (primary result)
All 10 immune genes differed significantly between groups at the spot level (all adj. p < 10⁻¹³, ★★★★). Two opposing signatures:

**Higher in TP53-mutant:**
- **CXCL13**: 31.5% spots expressing, mean 1.58 vs 12.5%, mean 0.58 (Δ +1.00) — broad chemokine presence
- **CD68**: 53.6% vs 45.5%, mean Δ +0.68 — myeloid-enriched microenvironment
- **CD4**: 41.0% vs 33.3%, mean Δ +0.45 — elevated T helper activity

**Higher in TP53 wild-type:**
- **CD274** (PD-L1): 13.3% vs 4.4%, mean Δ −0.41 — more IFN-γ-driven adaptive checkpoint upregulation
- **CD8A**, **GZMB**, **PRF1**: collectively lower in TP53-mutant — reduced cytotoxic T cell density and activity
- **TIGIT**, **FOXP3**, **CD19**: modestly but significantly lower in TP53-mutant

---

### Figure 3 — CXCL13 × T/NK Colocalization Slide Display
Per-patient LIANA colocalization scores are browsable via the patient dropdown. TP53-mutant patients (PAT_B_5WTQ8UP5, PAT_B_BXQ36W6A, PAT_B_CX5442GK) have high CXCL13 but lower T/NK density, suggesting CXCL13+ spots are spatially dissociated from T cell niches — inconsistent with canonical TLS organization.

---

### Biological Interpretation
TP53-mutant BLCA presents a **myeloid-enriched, cytotoxic-depleted spatial immune landscape**: elevated CD68+/CXCL13+ niches likely driven by macrophages or CAFs rather than TLS-organizing Tfh cells, coupled with reduced CD8+ effector infiltration and paradoxically lower PD-L1. This pattern is consistent with innate immune activation rather than adaptive anti-tumor immunity, which may partly explain the poor IO responses observed in this cohort (3/4 IO-treated BLCA patients had progressive disease).

**Important caveat**: only 3 TP53-mutant patients are available — spot-level results are highly powered (14,054 vs 54,315 spots), but patient-level confounders cannot be excluded without a larger cohort.
