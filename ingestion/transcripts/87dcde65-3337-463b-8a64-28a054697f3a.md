In MOSAIC high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma (OV; n = 7 patients), **Tumor Edge** shows the highest CD8+ T cell density, followed by **Stroma**, with **Tumor Islet** exhibiting the lowest abundance — based on spatial deconvolution fractions (`t_cd8_deconv_sample_level_custom1_frac`) per spot.

**Mean CD8+ T cell fractions by region (across samples):**
| Tumor Region | Mean fraction (range across patients) |
|---|---|
| **Tumor Edge** | ~0.026 (0.005 – 0.040) |
| **Stroma** | ~0.015 (0.007 – 0.032) |
| **Tumor Islet** | ~0.007 (0.002 – 0.021) |

**Statistical comparisons** (Wilcoxon, Bonferroni-adjusted):
- **Tumor Edge vs. Tumor Islet**: mean diff = +0.019 in favor of Tumor Edge; raw p = 0.017, adjusted p = 0.052 (borderline, does not reach significance after correction)
- **Stroma vs. Tumor Islet**: adjusted p = 0.109 (not significant)
- **Stroma vs. Tumor Edge**: adjusted p = 0.209 (not significant)

While none of the pairwise comparisons pass the adjusted significance threshold (p < 0.05), the consistent trend across all 7 patients places CD8+ T cell infiltration highest at the **Tumor Edge** (invasive margin), intermediate in the **Stroma**, and lowest within **Tumor Islets** — consistent with the well-known exclusion of cytotoxic T cells from the tumor core in ovarian cancer.

Analysis run on MOSAIC OV (high_grade_serous_carcinoma_ovary; 7 patients, baseline primary/metastatic tumor samples). Spatial deconvolution fractions for CD8+ T cells (`t_cd8_deconv_sample_level_custom1_frac`, sample-level model) were averaged per sample per tumor region (Stroma, Tumor Edge, Tumor Islet; 'Not Computed' spots excluded) and visualized as violin plots. Pairwise Wilcoxon tests with Bonferroni correction were used for statistical comparisons.
