## 1. Intratumoral Cell Type Composition (Stacked Bar)

Descriptive trends were observed but none reached statistical significance (Wilcoxon, all adjusted p-values = 1.0):

| Cell Type | TP53 Mutated (mean) | TP53 WT (mean) | Adj. p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lymphocytes | 125.7 | 211.2 | 1.0 (p=0.54) |
| Eosinophils | 6.1 | 20.8 | 1.0 (p=0.23) |
| Fibroblasts | 458 | 488.5 | 1.0 (p=0.73) |
| Plasmocytes | 3.0 | 3.7 | 1.0 (p=0.84) |
| Neutrophils | 10.9 | 11.5 | 1.0 (p=1.0) |
| Cancer cells | 2236 | 1929 | 1.0 (p=0.36) |

TP53-mutant slides numerically trended toward **lower lymphocyte and eosinophil densities** relative to TP53-wildtype tumors.

---

## 2. Spatial Immune Architecture Metrics (Boxplot)

| Metric | TP53 Mutated (mean) | TP53 WT (mean) | Adj. p-value |
|---|---|---|---|
| TILs diffusivity | 0.467 (n=1) | 0.298 (n=10) | 0.36 |
| TLS score | 0.602 (n=3) | 0.800 (n=12) | 0.63 |

- **TILs diffusivity** (spatial spread of tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes) was numerically higher in the single TP53-mutant slide, suggesting potentially more dispersed TIL architecture, but with n=1 this is uninformative.
- **TLS score** (tertiary lymphoid structure formation) trended lower in TP53-mutant tumors (0.60 vs. 0.80), which could suggest reduced organized lymphoid structure, but the difference is non-significant (p=0.63).

---

## Conclusion

No statistically significant difference in spatial immune architecture was detected between TP53-mutant and TP53-wildtype BLCA patients in MOSAIC. The cohort is too small (especially n=3 TP53-mutant) to draw firm conclusions. The numerically observed trends — lower intratumoral lymphocyte/eosinophil densities and lower TLS scores in TP53-mutant tumors — are biologically interesting hypotheses that warrant validation in a larger cohort (e.g., TCGA BLCA, n~400).

**Analysis 1 — Intratumoral Cell Type Composition (proportion_analysis, stacked_bar)**
Using H&E histomics density data for MOSAIC BLCA (15 patients baseline primary/metastatic). TP53 mutation status derived from WES. Wilcoxon tests applied across all cell types; none reached significance after Bonferroni correction (all adjusted p=1.0). Numerically, TP53-mutant slides showed lower lymphocyte (125.7 vs 211.2 cells/mm²) and eosinophil (6.1 vs 20.8) densities vs wildtype.

**Analysis 2 — Spatial Metrics: TILs Diffusivity & TLS Score (aggregation_analysis, boxplot)**
TP53-mutant tumors showed numerically higher TILs diffusivity (0.47 vs 0.30, p=0.18) and lower TLS score (0.60 vs 0.80, p=0.63) compared to TP53-wildtype, but neither difference was statistically significant. Very limited power due to n=3 mutant patients (n=1 for TILs diffusivity).
